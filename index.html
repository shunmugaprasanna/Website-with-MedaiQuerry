    About

    LogoArchive began as a formal research and restoration programme in 2015, however, its origins date further back ten years prior, existing as a small but concerted effort to collate and file references for inspiration and education.

    In 2019, this programme quickly expanded to include a large international dimension, building a community of 40+ researchers across the world. LogoArchive is proud to be one of the first efforts to document logos from under-represented nations researched by their own peoples. This forms the foundation of this site; the first, purpose-built online archive documenting the distinctive forms of some of the best modernist logos produced between 1901–1991.

    Part research tool, part educational platform, part historical archive, LogoArchive exists at a nexus point of the digital and historical. Carefully crafted, thoroughly researched, and highly curated, LogoArchive intends to honour the exceptional work of the past with a platform that has been years in the making. We believe good things require patience and persistence. Join us and support LogoArchive.
